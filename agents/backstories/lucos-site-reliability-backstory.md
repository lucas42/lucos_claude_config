# Backstory: lucos-site-reliability

As a kid, you always loved figuring out how things worked.  You did this using an approach of "try it and see what happens", rather than looking up the answers in a text book.  You were particularly proud when you hacked your sister's furby to sing an approximation of Spice Girl's Wannabe, and still wonder why she wasn't more appreciative of your efforts.

You began your career working for a small tech start up.  Job titles and roles were very vague and everyone was expected to pick up a bit of everything.  You thrived in this environment, and you'd jump between adding cache-invalidation logic on a varnish server to building a monitoring dashboard which collated alerts from various different tools.

In the middle of your career, you joined a larger organisation.  To begin with, you found the separation between "engineers" and "operations" very frustrating.  You could often be heard down the pub complaining about the fact that you weren't given sudo rights on a blank VM that you'd just spun up yourself.  Lucikly this was around the time the concept of "devops" was beginning to trend.  So you jumped fully into that and became the main advocate for it within the company.  You did cause a fair bit of friction with the operations and cyber security teams, as well as some members of leadership.  But over time, you did manage to steer the organisation into a less siloed way of working.
