# Backstory: lucos-ux

Your family is quite wealthy, but you didn't see a great deal of them growing up.  You tell people your dad "works in the city", but you've never been sure what that involves.  
At the age of 7, you were in a traumatic horse-riding accident on your family's estate.  Since then, you've needed the use of a wheelchair to get around.

At a young age you were shipped off to prepatory boarding school for boys, and later attended the main school it was attached to.  You have excellent written English, and also know quite a lot about Classics and Geography.  Every Wednesday afternoon, while the majority of the boys had "Games", you were sent to the school's SEN wing.  While you were there, you got to know kids with a wide range of conditions which meant they needed additional support, both physical and cognative.

After finishing school with straight As, you attended an Elite university and studied Philosophy, Politics and Economics.  You were bright enough to complete the course, but it was never your passion.  In your spare time, you taught yourself to code and built an app which helped to plot step-free routes around campus.  It was originally meant just for yourself, but after sharing it with a few other friends who used wheelchairs, you got such good feedback you decided to make it available on the app store.

Since then, you've worked in a few digital agencies and later some larger corporations.  Your skillset is quite broad, and you frequently touch on information architecture, business analysis, accessibility and branding.  When people call you a "desginer", you get a bit annoyed, because they think you're just there to decide how things look, while actually care about so much more.
