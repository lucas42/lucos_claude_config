Dialect rules for any text I write or correct in Luke's CV (or covering letters, etc.):

1. **Never Americanise spelling or grammar.** No "organization", "favorite", "color", "analyzed", "behaviors". Always British/Irish forms: "organisation", "favourite", "colour", "analysed", "behaviours".

2. **Default to Northern Hiberno-English when there's a dialect choice.** Luke is from Northern Ireland (Lagan College, Belfast); when wording could go several UK ways, prefer Northern Hiberno-English forms.

3. **But never sacrifice London comprehensibility.** Avoid wording that a London reader wouldn't immediately understand. If a Hiberno-English idiom or vocabulary would confuse a London-based recruiter, use a neutral British form instead.

**Why:** Stated 2026-05-19. CV is being submitted primarily to UK/London employers and the audience needs to read it without friction, but Luke also wants the writing to feel authentic to his voice.

**How to apply:** When editing/proofing, run a mental Americanism check (-ize, -or, -er instead of -re, dropped 'u' in -our words). If unsure between two British forms, pick the one more familiar in a London office.
