Every sentence in a cover letter must be comprehensible to a reader who has not yet seen Luke's CV.

**Why:** ATS flows commonly present cover letter and CV side-by-side. A meaningful fraction of recruiters open the cover letter first to decide whether to give the CV a careful read. For senior IC and leadership roles especially, hiring managers often read the cover letter as a "framing" document before scanning the CV. Stated 2026-05-20 after I produced a draft where the opener required the reader to have already parsed multiple bullets of CV context (specifically the "unbackfilled Principal Engineer responsibilities" framing) to understand what was being claimed.

**How to apply:**

- Before showing Luke a draft, read each sentence aloud as if the CV doesn't exist. If a sentence implies facts the reader needs to look up elsewhere, rewrite it.
- Acceptable: claims that can be taken at face value ("I led cyber security at the Financial Times", "I architected the FT's publishing platform").
- Not acceptable: claims that require the reader to assemble multiple facts from the CV to understand ("the unbackfilled Principal Engineer responsibilities for cyber security technical direction throughout my recent director-level roles" — requires the reader to already know there was a PE role, that it wasn't backfilled, that Luke kept doing it alongside the director job).
- The CV does the work of mapping out the chronology and the role-overlap nuance. The cover letter introduces the *story* in plain language.

**Pattern**: where the CV reader sees "Held the unbackfilled Principal Engineer responsibilities…", the cover letter reader sees "I've spent the last several years in a director-level cyber security role at the Financial Times, but kept the principal-engineer technical work alongside it rather than handing it off."
