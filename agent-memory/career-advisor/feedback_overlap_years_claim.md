When drafting any document where Luke claims years of experience across multiple domains (cover letter, CV summary, LinkedIn, interview answer), the **sum of all year-claims in that document must not exceed his total years working**, even if each individual claim is locally justifiable.

**Why:** Many of Luke's roles span multiple domains simultaneously. For example, the Principal Engineer (Reliability Engineering, Cyber Security, Observability & Edge Delivery) role from Feb 2018 – Mar 2022 can legitimately be counted as cyber security leadership (supporting a "5 years cyber" claim) *or* as software engineering / platform leadership (supporting an "X years software engineering" claim) — but not both inside the same document, because that would imply parallel tenures that didn't exist. Luke stated 2026-05-20 that he nearly submitted a cover letter where the year-claims across domains added up to more than his actual working tenure, and that felt misleading even though each individual claim was defensible.

**How to apply:**

1. Before finalising any draft, total up every "[N] years of [domain]" claim in the document.
2. Compare against Luke's total years working (currently ~15 years, from Nov 2010 onwards).
3. If domains overlap (e.g. cyber + software engineering overlapping during the 2018–2022 Principal Engineer period), the sum across overlapping domains must not include the overlap twice.
4. If the totals would exceed actual tenure, pick which domain-framing each role-period counts for in this document and stick to it.
5. The same role-period can be framed differently in *different* documents (e.g. cyber in one cover letter, software engineering in another) — that's fine, because each reader only sees one document.

**Practical rules from Luke's stated preference:**
- For JDs where cyber experience is a strength: count Principal Engineer years as cyber → "5 years cyber security leadership".
- For JDs that want software engineering experience and less cyber: count Principal Engineer years as software engineering → "3 years cyber security leadership" and the PE years feed software engineering tenure instead.
